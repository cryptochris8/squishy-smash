# Squishy Smash: Meet the Squishies — Elevation Plan v1

*Synthesis of 5 parallel specialist reviews: UX, industry research, visual/UI design, information architecture, and copy audit. Produced 2026-04-25.*

---

## North star

The current draft is a list of 48 character cards with paragraphs next to them. Every reviewer reached the same diagnosis through different lenses:

> The book treats the cards as the artifact and the pages as scaffolding around them. Premium character books treat the *book itself* as the artifact — a field guide narrated by a fictional voice, with a per-character data schema, a region map, a back-of-book collection tracker, and visually distinct rarity tiers. The card is the photograph; the book is the museum.

This plan reorganizes the book around four shifts:

1. **Featured-24, not all-48 hero-treated.** 24 characters get full hero pages; the other 24 live in a single back-matter gallery. Solves the "contact sheet" feeling at the structural level.
2. **A narrator voice (the Squishkeeper) frames the whole thing as a field guide.** Replaces "welcome to a bouncy world" with "here is what I know about every squishy."
3. **A 6-field per-character schema** (vs. the current 3 fields) gives each character genuine information density.
4. **A 10-template visual library** with per-pack visual signatures replaces today's single "card-left-bio-right" skeleton.

## Per-character data schema (6 fields)

Replaces the current 3-field schema (name / pack / 2-trait bullets / flavor line). New schema:

| Field | Words | Why |
|---|---:|---|
| **Name** | 1–4 | Required |
| **Pack + Rarity badge** | — | Combined header line; rarity becomes a star count (★ = common, ★★ = rare, ★★★ = epic, ★★★★ = mythic). Scannable, no text. |
| **Squishkeeper says…** (italic, narrator voice) | ~14 | Replaces flat "intro." Always italic; always narrator-voice; never asks a question. |
| **First spotted at** (location anchor to page-4 map) | 3–5 | "First spotted at the Pudding Hills." Anchors character to geography. |
| **Signature squish** (the *thing it does*, with sound effect) | ~10 | "A slow, gentle puff-and-settle. *pmf.*" Replaces the two flat traits. |
| **Pack-mate** (one named friend) | 1–4 | Tiny social graph; turns the cast into a community; gives reread hooks. |

**Total per character:** 35–45 words. **Per spread (2 characters):** 70–90 words. Inside the brief's 30–90 band ✓.

### Worked example — Soft Dumpling

> **Soft Dumpling** · Squishy Foods · ★ *common*
>
> *Squishkeeper says:* "A cozy little friend whose every bounce leaves a puff of warm joy."
>
> **First spotted at:** the Pudding Hills.
> **Signature squish:** a slow, gentle puff-and-settle. *pmf.*
> **Pack-mate:** Jelly Bun.

### Worked example — Mythic Plush Familiar (T10 finale, no bullets)

> **Mythic Plush Familiar** · Creepy-Cute · ★★★★ *mythic*
>
> When a squishy gets lost, a soft pawprint shows up in the dust. Then another. Then another.
>
> *Someone is always coming back for them.*

Mythic copy intentionally drops the bullet schema — fairy-tale paragraphs only, with silence (`*hush.*`) where commons get sound effects.

## Lore framework — The Squishkeeper

A gentle off-page narrator. Never appears in art. Speaks only in italics. The book frames itself as their personal field guide.

**Page 3 — Letter from the Squishkeeper (full copy, ~80 words):**

> Long ago, the very first wobble rolled across the Squishy World — and someone had to keep track of every wonderful squish that followed. That someone is the Squishkeeper. No one is quite sure where the Squishkeeper lives (somewhere soft, probably) or what the Squishkeeper looks like (probably round). What we know is this: every squishy that bounces, ripples, glows, or giggles has a page in the Squishkeeper's book. This is that book.

Lock this 80 words as canon for future volumes.

## Map of the Squishy World — Page 4

Three labeled regions (one per pack), aligned 1:1 with pack groupings so the geography is unambiguous:

- **Pudding Hills** → Squishy Foods region
- **Goo Coast** → Goo & Fidgets region
- **Moonlit Hollow** → Creepy-Cute Creatures region

Plus 4–6 sub-landmarks per region used as "First spotted at" anchors:
- Pudding Hills: Sprinkle Cliffs, Dumpling Dell, Mochi Meadows, Syrup River
- Goo Coast: Bubble Bay, Stretch Tide, Plasma Shore, Aurora Reef
- Moonlit Hollow: Whisper Woods, Star Pond, Crescent Cave, Cuddle Glade

This becomes the back-pocket geography for sticker books, sequels, and game tie-ins. Volume 2 can add sub-regions without retconning anything.

## Voice + copy guide (locked)

### The Squishy Smash voice DOES

1. Use sound words as punctuation. "*Boing.* That's Jelly Bun."
2. Talk to the reader. "Have you met…?", "Give it a poke."
3. Use short sentences. Then shorter ones. Like this.
4. Describe **one specific thing** per character (rosy cheeks, glitter footprints, dense thump) instead of three vague feelings.
5. Use lowercase sparingly when it serves rhythm — e.g., "*soft. soft. soft.*"

### The Squishy Smash voice DOES NOT

1. Stack three marketing adjectives ("glossy, wiggly, and always…").
2. Use abstract emotions as descriptors ("magical," "satisfying," "comforting").
3. Use words a 5-year-old can't define ("prismatic," "rhythmic," "celestial," "singularity," "rebound").
4. Use em dashes for pauses. Kids read periods.
5. End on a marketing line ("ready to bounce, wobble, and shine!").

### Sentence-shape rotation (avoid the "48 product descriptions" trap)

Rotate across these 5 shapes so no two consecutive bios sound the same:

1. **Question hook:** "Have you met Jelly Bun? *Wobble, wobble.* Now you have."
2. **Sound-first:** "*POP!* That was Cream Puff saying hi."
3. **Two-word punch + tag:** "Slide. Splat. That's just how Syrup Cube says hello."
4. **Imperative invite:** "Give Peach Mochi a poke. See that little puff of pink? That's a sigh of joy."
5. **Repeating-word anchor:** "Soft Dumpling is soft. Soft on top. Soft on the bottom. Soft all the way through."

### Sound effect bank (lock 12+ for the 24 featured)

| Pack | Sound effects |
|---|---|
| Foods | *pmf*, *POOF!*, *thwump*, *sssslip*, *tink-tink-tink*, *wibble-wobble*, *POP!* |
| Goo | *sploink*, *WHOOMPF*, *krrrk-zap!*, *ZZZAP!*, *bloop*, *splatch* |
| Creatures | *shhhhh-splash*, *boo-boo-boop*, *tap-tap-tap*, *whisper-whisper*, *tiny-tiny-roar* |

Mythic copy uses **silence**: `*…*` or `*hush.*` Where commons BOING, mythics breathe.

**Phase 2 — Visual system tokens + frame system**
1. Extend `PALETTE`, add `PACK_BG_GRADIENT`, `RARITY_RING`, shadow/glow token dicts

4. Implement per-pack background gradient renderer
5. Bake the 3 pack texture PNGs (sprinkles / bubbles / moondust) — once, ship in repo
6. Tests: render each rarity tier, snapshot test the rarity-ring colors

**Phase 3 — Page-template library**
1. Implement T1–T11 templates as functions in `build_interior.py` (replacing the current 7-ish templates)
2. Refactor `PAGE_ORDER` to the new 32-page sequence
3. Tests: assert each template fits its page word budget; visual diff snapshots for each template

**Phase 4 — Decorative atoms + cover rebuild**
1. Source/draw 8 decorative atoms (folio, rule, drop-cap, 3 pack ornaments, mythic badge, trait chip, sparkle set)
2. Rebuild the cover wordmark with layered fills + drop shadow + sparkle accents
3. Asymmetric hero trio (rotation + overlap + z-stack shadows)
4. Add ambient art band: pack-zone vertical bands across the cover

**Phase 5 — Map of the Squishy World + back matter**
1. Source/commission the page-4 map illustration with 12 labeled landmarks
2. Implement page 31 (24-thumbnail gallery) and page 32 (48-cell tracker checklist)
3. Tests: assert all 48 character thumbnails render on pages 31–32

**Phase 6 — Proof + Polish**
1. Re-run smoke tests + page-count + dimension tests
2. Open the rendered PDF page-by-page, hit every checklist item in `book/PROOF_CHECKLIST.md`
3. Order an author proof copy from KDP

Phases 1–3 lift the perceived quality the most. Phases 4–5 close the gap to "premium."
