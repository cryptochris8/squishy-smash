# 04 — Audio and VFX Plan

## Audio Vision
Audio is one of the main reasons this game can outperform a generic smash game.
The player should hear a rich stack of textures:
- soft squish
- wet pop
- rubber stretch
- splat impact
- goo drip tail
- tiny particle fizz

## Sound Philosophy
Do not rely on one repeated “pop” effect.
Every interaction should feel layered and slightly varied.

## Audio Layers Per Hit
### Light Hit
- soft poke
- tiny gel movement
- micro splat

### Medium Hit
- fuller squish
- pressure squeak
- wall slap

### Burst Event
- pressure build
- pop / rupture
- goo splash
- particle sparkle or sticky droplets

## ElevenLabs Usage Ideas
ElevenLabs is best used here for:
- stylized UI voice lines
- event announcer bites
- subtle comedic character reactions
- trailer voiceovers
- meme pack intros

Example voice line packs:
- “Squish combo!”
- “That one popped hard.”
- “New goo unlocked.”
- “Messy. Very messy.”

Do not overuse voice lines during gameplay or they will interrupt the sensory loop.

## Sound Source Recommendations
Combine:
- custom-generated sound design
- recorded household Foley
- edited textures for squeeze / slime / wet slap / gel movement
- pitch and timing randomization

## Audio Variation Rules
For each frequently used event, include at least:
- 4 light hit variants
- 4 medium hit variants
- 3 burst variants
- 2 goo drip tails

Randomize slightly:
- pitch: ±3–6%
- volume: minor range
- start offset: tiny variation if layered

## Arts.io Usage Ideas
Use Arts.io for:
- concept art directions
- marketing visuals
- creepy-cute creature ideation
- goo burst key art
- store creatives
- social ad stills

## Visual FX Goals
The player should see immediate feedback in these categories:
- deformation squash / stretch
- particle burst
- sticky decals on walls
- droplets / sprays
- tiny recoil motion
- brief screen shake on big burst
- haptic pulse on strong hits

## Particle Types
### Goo Burst
- blobs
- droplets
- sticky arcs

### Powder / Fizz
- tiny sparkle or dust puffs for certain objects

### Burst Core
- central pop ring
- splash chunk particles

## Decal System
A light decal system will help the play area feel messy and alive.
Recommended decal presets:
- pink jelly splat
- green goo smear
- orange soup splash
- glitter gel residue

Keep decals temporary or capped to preserve performance.

## Color Strategy
Use bright, readable colors with enough contrast for social clips.
Recommended palette directions:
- candy pink
- electric lime
- jelly blue
- creamy beige
- toxic slime green for selected packs

## Haptics
For iPhone, haptics should be a major part of the feel.
Map:
- light hit → light tap
- stronger slam → medium impact
- burst → heavy impact with short screen shake

## Trailer Audio Direction
Marketing clips should emphasize:
- dry silence for 0.3–0.5 sec
- sudden big pop
- layered splat
- fast text or VO hook

That contrast helps clips stop scrolling.
