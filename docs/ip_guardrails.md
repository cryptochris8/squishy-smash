# 08 — IP and Trend Guardrails

## Purpose
This game should ride attention waves without creating avoidable legal or platform risk.

## Main Rule
Use **trend energy**, not direct branded character copies.

## Do Not Do
- Do not use the names of famous toy brands or characters in the shipped game unless rights are secured.
- Do not mimic a protected character so closely that it reads as a direct knockoff.
- Do not build your entire title or App Store metadata around one branded meme.

## Safe Direction
Translate trends into original categories.

### Example Conversions
- “Labubu-like craze” → **creepy-cute collectible monster pack**
- “viral squishy dumpling toy” → **squishy food burst pack**
- “internet brainrot vibe” → **chaos mode / absurd announcer pack**

## Why This Matters
Trend-chasing works best when the code and art can outlive one week of internet hype.
If you build around a flexible style bucket, you can keep shipping content even after one specific meme cools off.

## Naming Rule
Use original names for all in-game content.
Examples:
- Gobble Puff
- Soupy Blob
- Wink Fang
- Jelly Gremlin
- Gloop Dumpling

## Visual Rule
Create original silhouettes, color palettes, eyes, teeth, accessories, and proportions.
Do not trace trend objects too literally.

## Marketing Rule
Market the app around durable value:
- satisfying
- squishy
- ASMR
- weird cute objects
- antistress chaos

Then let social posts reference the broader trend energy without shipping infringing content.

## Practical Benefit
This protects:
- App Store longevity
- monetization options
- future expansions
- brand credibility
